Lake Chuzenji

Situated in Nikko City, Tochigi Prefecture, this mountain lake was formed by an eruption of Mount Nantai. The scenery is breathtaking, particularly in autumn when the surrounding mountains turn vibrant shades of red and gold. It’s one of the best nature-viewing spots in the Kanto region.

Red Brick

Located in Yokohama City, Kanagawa Prefecture, this historic building was originally used as an import-export warehouse in the early 20th century. Today, it has been transformed into a popular tourist destination with shops, cafés, and an open event space especially lively during the Christmas Market and the Oktoberfest celebrations.

Great Buddha

SFound at Kōtoku-in Temple in Kamakura City, Kanagawa Prefecture, this bronze outdoor statue stands about 13 meters tall and dates back to the 13th century. It is one of Japan’s most iconic Buddhist monuments and a major cultural symbol.
